Spencer's story

Written by a fellow traveller.

— Spencer Hall, Brighton

I spent more than twenty-five years inside a loop of harmful habits. My world was horse racing, and gambling was the loudest habit of them all — but it wasn't the only one. Recovery, when it came, didn't arrive through willpower alone. It arrived through making things.

In Barcelona I built a furniture workshop around yakisugi, the Japanese craft of charring wood — taking something through fire and finding it stronger and more beautiful on the other side. Alongside it I grew a Meetup community of hundreds, and in the workshop I taught the craft to others — watching creativity transform people, because it was quietly transforming me.

Back home in Brighton I trained as a Recovery Coach Professional — designated by CCAR through Recovery Coach Academy UK — and shaped everything I'd learned into a framework I could put in your hands. That framework became C.R.E.A.T.E., and these ten manuals are the result, written the way I taught in the workshop: beside you, not above you. Since then I've delivered recovery coaching within Reframe Coaching's gambling aftercare programme, and served on the development panel of the Gambling Lived Experience Network (GLEN).

Credentials. Recovery Coach Professional (RCP), internationally accredited through CCAR via Recovery Coach Academy UK. Member of the International Association of Recovery Coach Professionals (IARCP).

Questions

Asked, and answered honestly.

Is this therapy?

No. The manuals and coaching are structured self-help and recovery coaching, built on lived experience and the C.R.E.A.T.E. framework. They aren't medical, psychiatric or therapeutic care — and they work well alongside it. If you're in crisis, please contact your GP or the Samaritans (116 123) first.

Is Coachall a charity?

Not yet. Coachall is an independent initiative led by lived experience. Some things are free — the Honest Check-In and the starter guides — and the manuals and coaching are paid. That paid work funds the development of free peer-led recovery sessions in Brighton & Hove, built to the standards of the College of Lived Experience Recovery Organisations (CLERO). A formal structure is being explored as that work grows.

Is there anything free?

Yes. The Honest Check-In is a free download, and the Start Here guide — the complete introduction to the series — is a free download right here on the site. Free peer-led sessions in Brighton are in development — the mailing list hears first. Beyond Coachall, the Other Support page lists excellent free services across the city.

Do you refer people to treatment?

Actively, yes. Coachall isn't a treatment service, and no single pathway fits everyone — treatment, mutual aid, coaching, community, faith, or a combination. The Other Support page lists the local and national services I point people towards, including gambling-specific help, the NHS, mutual aid meetings and debt advice.

What exactly do I get when I buy a manual?

A beautifully formatted PDF you download immediately and keep forever. Read it on any device or print it. Each one is a complete, self-contained journey through the C.R.E.A.T.E. framework applied to its subject — with exercises, check-ins and things to make, not just things to read.

Which manual should I start with?

The one your life is asking for. If you're not sure, take the free Honest Check-In first — it was built to answer exactly this question. Still unsure? Email me, I read everything.

I'm not creative. Will this still work for me?

Yes — this framework was built by someone who hadn't made anything in decades before it saved him. "Creative" here means small, doable acts: a walk done differently, a sentence written honestly, a shelf sanded smooth. No talent required, only willingness.

Can I get a refund?

Yes. If a manual isn't what you hoped, email me within 14 days and I'll refund you — see the refund policy for the details.

Do you work with gambling problems specifically?

Gambling was my loudest habit, and the Sobriety & Recovery manual was forged there. Coaching clients affected by gambling are very welcome — the free discovery call is the right first step.

In crisis right now? Please contact your GP, call the Samaritans free on 116 123, or emergency services. Coachall supports recovery — it isn't a crisis service.
