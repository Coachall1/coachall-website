The gap this fills

Three things most recovery services are missing.

Structured content peers can run

Most groups run on the facilitator's improvisation. C.R.E.A.T.E. gives trained staff and volunteers a fixed eight-session arc with full plans, timed agendas and failure modes — so quality doesn't depend on who's in the room.

Something to make, not just discuss

Every session produces a physical artefact — a support map, a timeline, an anchor canvas, a promise card, a sealed letter. The work leaves the room in participants' pockets and keeps working between sessions.

Evidence a commissioner accepts

WEMWBS baseline and closing scores, attendance, completion and fidelity data — compiled for you into a two-page outcomes report, per cohort, with the instrument's required acknowledgements handled correctly.

The programme

Six letters. Eight sessions. One fixed spine.

The framework's letters never change; the words are the recovery set. The order is load-bearing: safety before depth, structure before surrender, self-trust before growth.

The evidence posture

We don't say "proven". Here's what we say instead.

"Faith is not a procurement category. This programme shows you its mechanism, its measurement, and its honest numbers — including the cohorts that come back flat."
  • Built from evidenced components. Peer-delivered recovery support: systematic reviews find reduced relapse, better treatment retention and stronger social connection. Making-based, arts activity: the WHO's review of over 3,000 studies found a major role for the arts in preventing and managing ill health. Structured expressive writing: four decades of research on psychological and physical health benefits.
  • Measured with a UK-standard instrument. WEMWBS — used across UK public mental health and in substance-misuse evaluations — administered at Sessions 1 and 8, with change reported honestly against the published meaningful-improvement threshold.
  • Reported cohort by cohort. Enrolled, completed, attendance, mean change, percentage reaching meaningful improvement — plus onward referrals and what was learned. A flat cohort is reported with learning, not hidden.
  • Safeguarded in writing. The facilitator manual carries a complete boundaries and safeguarding protocol aligned to the Care Act and Making Safeguarding Personal — escalation routes, disclosure scripts and confidentiality limits stated to participants in Session 1.

Commissioners ask

The questions your board will ask, answered.

Does this replace treatment?

No — and the manual says so to facilitators in writing. C.R.E.A.T.E. is structured peer support that sits alongside clinical and keywork provision. Participants whose needs exceed the group are warmly referred into your existing pathways, with their place in the group kept open.

We already have a programme. Why take on another?

You don't have to replace anything. Services typically run C.R.E.A.T.E. where they have no structured group offer, or as a step-down after treatment. It can also be white-labelled under your brand — the fidelity standards stay, the name becomes yours.

Who facilitates, and what about safeguarding?

Your staff or trained volunteers, using your safer-recruitment and DBS arrangements. The manual's safeguarding protocol aligns with the Care Act and Making Safeguarding Personal, with a defined escalation route into your service's named safeguarding lead — it adapts to your policies, never around them.

What exactly do we get to show our commissioners?

A two-page outcomes report per cohort: enrolment and completion, attendance, WEMWBS change against the meaningful-improvement threshold, anonymised participant voices, onward referrals and learning. The instrument's acknowledgement requirements are handled correctly, and missing data is reported as missing.

Can we review the materials before committing?

Yes. The framework overview and pilot proposal are available on request. The full facilitator manual is shared under a simple NDA once we've had a first call — the intellectual property is registered and licensed, not sold.
