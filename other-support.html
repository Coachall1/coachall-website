Signposting

Other support in Brighton & Hove

Coachall is one part of a recovery pathway — never the whole map. These are services I trust and point people towards. Multiple pathways, no wrong doors: whatever works for you is the right place to start.

My world

Gambling harm — specific support

Twenty-five years of my life was lived here. These are the doors worth knocking on first if gambling is the thing you’re carrying.

National Gambling Helpline (GamCare)

0808 8020 133 — free, 24/7, phone or live chat. The single best first call if gambling is hurting you or someone you love.

NHS Southern Gambling Service

Specialist NHS treatment for gambling harm, covering Sussex. Self-referral online, open from age 17. Free and confidential.

nhs.uk/gambling

Gamblers Anonymous

Twelve-step fellowship for gambling recovery — meetings in Brighton and online, free.

gamblersanonymous.org.uk

SMART Recovery — Brighton

Practical, evidence-based meetings that work for gambling as much as substances — no labels, no steps. Thursdays 10:00–11:30am, Holland Road Baptist Church, Brighton. Free, just turn up.

smartrecovery.org.uk

For families & affected others

GamFam — lived-experience network for families. Gordon Moody Friends & Family — online weekly support for partners, parents and friends.

Practical blocks, today

GamStop — self-exclusion from UK gambling sites and apps. Gamban — blocking software for your devices. Your bank — most UK banks can block gambling transactions in their app. Three barriers, ten minutes each.

Treatment

Drug, alcohol & mental health services

Commissioned, clinical and free at the point of use.

Change Grow Live — Brighton & Hove Recovery Service

01273 731 900 — the commissioned drug and alcohol treatment service for the city. One-to-one support, groups, prescribing, family & carers team. Open to any adult in Brighton & Hove.

changegrowlive.org

Brighton & Hove Wellbeing Service

Free NHS mental health support — counselling, CBT, self-referral, for adults registered with a city GP.

brightonandhovewellbeing.org

BHT Sussex

Addiction pathway services across the city — detox support and recovery projects (via CGL), plus Move On, which accepts self-referrals.

Peer & mutual aid

People who’ve been there, meeting weekly

Kennedy Street Recovery Hub

Peer-led recovery charity in Brighton — open to anyone 18+ recovering from any addiction, gambling included. Peer support, recovery coaching, workshops, sober socials, volunteering. Helpline and in-person hub.

Alcoholics Anonymous

Meetings throughout Brighton & Hove, daily, free.

aa.org.uk

Narcotics Anonymous

Meetings throughout Sussex, free.

ukna.org

Money & debt

Where gambling harm cuts deepest

Debt is usually the first emergency and the last thing people talk about. These services deal with it without judgement.

Money Advice Plus

Brighton-based charity — specialist debt and welfare benefits advice projects for people in Brighton & Hove, including those who find mainstream advice hard to access. Free and confidential.

National Debtline

0808 808 4000 — free, independent debt advice by phone and webchat.

Citizens Advice Brighton & Hove

Free advice on debt, benefits, housing and work — online, by phone and in person.

Creative & community

Recovery you can make and sing

Cascade Creative Recovery

Free creative community at the Brighthelm Centre, North Road — choir, art, yoga, drama and more, built by and for people in recovery. Kindred spirits to everything C.R.E.A.T.E. stands for.
